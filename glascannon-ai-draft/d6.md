### §1. Purpose and Scope

This document defines the **Human-AI Interaction (HAI) Card**:
its structure, its auto-generation algorithm, its mapping to
publisher compliance requirements, and its relationship to the
cryptographic provenance chain defined in D-5.

The HAI Card is the **primary human-readable disclosure artifact**
produced by every factory run. It is the document a journal
editor, a peer reviewer, a JAIGP screener, or a member of the
public reads to understand — without cryptographic tooling,
without inspecting hash chains, without reading prompts — exactly
how a piece of content was produced and where the boundaries
between human and machine contribution lie.

**What this document decides:**
- The exact Markdown template for the HAI Card.
- The auto-generation algorithm from `FactoryState` and
  `ProvenanceManifest` (D-2 §10, D-2 §7.3).
- The mapping between HAI Card fields and publisher-specific
  compliance requirements (Elsevier, Springer Nature, Wiley,
  Taylor & Francis, JAIGP).
- The conditions under which HAI Card fields are populated
  differently (e.g., `NO_OUTPUT` runs, human-rescue runs,
  provenance-disabled runs).
- The relationship between the HAI Card and Aletheia's published
  HAI Card examples.

**What this document does NOT decide:**
- The exact text of the security guarantee (verbatim from D-1 §9).
- The Pydantic schemas (D-2).
- Prompt text (D-3).
- Deployment configuration (D-9).

**Key upstream constraints:**
- D-0 §9.2: The HAI Card is a mandatory output artifact for
  every completed run.
- D-1 §10: The HAI Card must include `scientific_review_status`
  and `configuration_overrides` fields, plus the honest security
  guarantee from D-1 §9 as boilerplate.
- D-2 §10: Defines `HAICardData` as the intermediate data
  structure used to render the Markdown template.
- D-5 §11: When provenance is disabled, the HAI Card must include
  a prominent warning.

### §2. Lineage: From Aletheia's HAI Cards to Ours

The HAI Card concept originates directly from Feng et al.
(2026b, §6.2):

> "Inspired by the established concept of 'model cards' used to
> describe the architectures and limitations of machine learning
> models, the HAI Card is a structured, tabular disclosure template
> designed to be appended directly to academic publications."

The Aletheia papers provide three concrete HAI Card examples
(for Feng26, LeeSeo26, and BKKKZ26 in §6.2 of 2602.10177v3)
and one embedded directly in the FirstProof report
(2602.21201v3, page 2). These examples share a common structure:
```plaintext
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│           Human-AI Interaction Card               │
├──────────────┬───────────────────────────────────┤
│ Action       │ Description                       │
│ Actor        │                                   │
├──────────────┼───────────────────────────────────┤
│ ...          │ ...                               │
├──────────────┴───────────────────────────────────┤
│ Raw prompts and outputs: [public repository URL] │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
**Key design principles extracted from Aletheia's examples:**

| Principle | Source | Our Adaptation |
|---|---|---|
| Each row represents a single *interaction turn* — one actor (Human or AI) performing one action. | All three Aletheia HAI Cards | Preserved. Our card has one row per cycle, plus rows for the human brief and the human review gate. |
| The actor is always identified as either "Human" or a specific model name ("Aletheia", "Gemini 3 Deep Think"). | All three examples | Extended. We identify the actor as "Human", the generator model name, or the verifier model name. |
| The card links to a public repository containing raw prompts and outputs. | All three examples; also 2602.21201v3 page 2 | Extended. We link to the workspace directory (or a published version of it), which contains the full provenance chain. |
| The card is short enough to fit on a single page. | All three examples are ≤ 10 rows | Preserved for typical runs (1–5 cycles). Long runs produce longer cards but retain the same structure. |
| The card does NOT attempt to evaluate the scientific quality of the output. | Implicit in all examples | Explicit. We add a `scientific_review_status` field that always starts as "PENDING". |

**Where our card extends beyond Aletheia's:**

Aletheia's HAI Cards describe interactions between human
mathematicians and DeepMind's proprietary models, within an
institutional context where the humans are named researchers
at top universities who take personal accountability for the
results. Our factory operates in a fundamentally different
trust context:

1. **No institutional reputation.** The operator may be an
   anonymous independent researcher. The card must compensate
   for the absence of institutional credibility.
2. **No human mathematical contribution.** In Aletheia's cards,
   the human provides the mathematical problem and sometimes
   key ideas. In our factory, the human provides only the
   research brief. The card must make this asymmetry visible.
3. **Automated verification, not expert evaluation.** Aletheia's
   outputs were evaluated by panels of named mathematicians.
   Our outputs are evaluated by an LLM Verifier. The card must
   state this clearly and honestly.
4. **The "slop" designation.** The card must carry the factory's
   honest self-assessment: this is highly polished slop, not
   verified research.

### §4. Section-by-Section Specification

#### 4.1 Disclaimer Block

The disclaimer block is the most prominent text in the card.
It is rendered in bold and carries the factory's honest
self-assessment.

**Default disclaimer (all runs):**
THIS DOCUMENT WAS PRODUCED BY AN AUTONOMOUS AI PIPELINE
("antiphoria slop-research-factory"). NO HUMAN HAS VERIFIED
THE SCIENTIFIC CLAIMS CONTAINED HEREIN.
The term "slop" is a deliberate, load-bearing engineering
designation. It indicates that the output exhibits the surface
structure of academic writing while lacking the epistemic
grounding that makes real research real. Citations may be
hallucinated. Logical steps may be invalid. Claims may be
unfounded.
The complete reasoning chain — including all prompts, all model
responses, all  traces, and all citation verification
results — is sealed in a cryptographic provenance chain and
available for independent audit.
DO NOT CITE THIS DOCUMENT AS "VERIFIED RESEARCH."
DO NOT CITE THIS DOCUMENT AS "CRYPTOGRAPHICALLY CERTIFIED."
The provenance chain verifies PROCESS, not TRUTH.
**Additional disclaimer when provenance is disabled:**
⚠ PROVENANCE WAS DISABLED FOR THIS RUN.
No cryptographic seals were generated. The generation process
is NOT auditable. This output carries NO tamper-evidence
guarantees. See §7 below.
**Additional disclaimer when human rescue was triggered:**
ℹ This run exceeded its automated revision budget and was
routed to a human rescue queue. See §5 for details of the
human intervention.
**Additional disclaimer for NO_OUTPUT runs:**
ℹ The factory determined that it could not produce a
meaningful draft for the provided research brief. The
NO_OUTPUT declaration is itself a sealed artifact in the
provenance chain. See the Generator's explanation below.

#### 4.4 Human Review Status

This field is the single most important piece of information
for anyone deciding whether to trust the output.

**Default value (no human review):**
```plaintext

PENDING — No human domain expert has verified the scientific
claims in this document.
The automated Verifier (gemini-2.5-flash) returned a CORRECT
verdict with confidence 0.83, based on:


Structural checks (deterministic)


Citation verification via Crossref and Semantic Scholar
(deterministic + LLM-assessed)


Logical soundness assessment (LLM-only, confidence ceiling 0.6)


Automated verification is NOT equivalent to human peer review.
See D-4 (Verification Taxonomy) for a full accounting of what
the Verifier can and cannot assess.
**When a human rescue occurred:**
HUMAN INTERVENTION OCCURRED
Reviewer: {reviewer_id or "Anonymous"}
Decision: {decision}
Comment: {comment or "No comment provided."}
{if decision == "approve_as_is"}
The human reviewer approved the current draft without
modifications. This constitutes a human endorsement of the
content, but the scope and depth of the review are unknown
to the factory.
{/if}
{if decision == "revise_manually"}
The human reviewer manually edited the draft. The edited
version was sealed as a HUMAN_GATE step in the provenance
chain. The human's modifications are visible in the diff
between the last automated draft and the final output.
{/if}
{if decision == "abort"}
The human reviewer terminated the run. The output is
classified as NO_OUTPUT.
{/if}
```

**Why is human_review_status never auto-set to "REVIEWED"?**

The factory cannot verify that a human actually read the
output carefully. Merely clicking "approve" in the rescue
queue does not constitute review. The `human_review_status`
field is set to "PENDING" by the factory and can only be
upgraded by a human editing the HAI Card directly — which
itself would be a modification to a sealed artifact. If the
human edits the HAI Card, they should re-seal it and note
the modification in the provenance chain.

This is intentionally conservative. Per D-1 §6
(Non-Defense 4): "The provenance chain is content-agnostic.
It seals bytes. It does not understand mathematics, logic,
or empirical truth."

### §6. Auto-Generation Algorithm

The HAI Card is generated during the FINALIZE node (D-5 §8),
after the manifest is compiled but before the manifest seal.
```plaintext
pseudocode:
def generate_hai_card(state: FactoryState,
manifest: ProvenanceManifest) -> str:
"""
Generate the HAI Card Markdown from state and manifest.
Returns the rendered Markdown string.
"""
  # 1. Construct HAICardData (D-2 §10)
  card_data = HAICardData(
      run_id = state.run_id,
      factory_version = FACTORY_VERSION,
      brief_thesis = state.brief["thesis"],
      brief_constraints = state.brief.get("constraints"),
      brief_key_references = state.brief.get("key_references"),
      brief_domain = state.brief.get("domain"),
      cycles = extract_cycles(state, manifest),
      final_verdict = extract_final_verdict(manifest),
      final_confidence = extract_final_confidence(manifest),
      total_cycles = state.cycle_count,
      total_inference_seconds = compute_total_inference(state),
      human_review_status = determine_review_status(state),
      configuration_overrides = manifest.configuration_overrides,
      manifest_path = "./chain/manifest.json",
      security_statement = SECURITY_STATEMENT_TEXT
  )

  # 2. Render the template
  md = render_template(HAI_CARD_TEMPLATE, card_data, manifest)

  # 3. Write to workspace
  write_file(f"{state.workspace}/hai_card.md", md)

  return md
def extract_cycles(state, manifest) -> list[dict]:
"""
Walk the verdict_history and messages to reconstruct
the chronological cycle table.
"""
cycles = []
for msg in state.messages:
cycle_num = compute_cycle_from_step(msg["step_index"])
cycles.append({
"cycle": cycle_num,
"actor": msg["role"],
"model": msg["model"],
"output_words": count_words(msg)
if msg["role"] != "verifier" else None,
"think_tokens": msg["token_counts"].get("think"),
"verdict": extract_verdict_for_step(
msg["step_index"], manifest) or None,
"confidence": extract_confidence_for_step(
msg["step_index"], manifest) or None,
"critique_count": count_critiques_for_step(
msg["step_index"], state),
"citations_found": count_citations_for_cycle(
cycle_num, state),
"citations_verified": count_verified_for_cycle(
cycle_num, state),
"citations_failed": count_failed_for_cycle(
cycle_num, state)
})
  # Insert HUMAN_GATE rows if present
  for gate in find_human_gates(manifest):
      cycles.insert(
          gate["position"],
          format_human_gate_row(gate)
      )

  return cycles
def determine_review_status(state) -> str:
"""
The review status is ALWAYS initialized to PENDING.
It is upgraded only if a HUMAN_GATE step exists
with decision == "approve_as_is" or "revise_manually".
"""
for msg in state.messages:
if msg.get("role") == "human_gate":
decision = msg.get("decision")
if decision in ("approve_as_is", "revise_manually"):
return (
f"HUMAN INTERVENTION OCCURRED — "
f"Decision: {decision}"
)
return (
"PENDING — No human domain expert has verified "
"the scientific claims."
)
```

### §7. Publisher Compliance Mapping

The HAI Card is designed to be a superset of the disclosure
requirements imposed by all major publishers as of April 2026.
This section maps specific publisher requirements to the HAI
Card sections that satisfy them.

#### 7.1 Publisher Policy Summary

| Publisher | AI Text Policy | Disclosure Requirement | Image Policy | Peer Review AI Ban |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Elsevier** | Aid only; no unedited AI output as final text | Mandatory separate "AI declaration statement" | No AI-generated images except as methodology | Yes |
| **Springer Nature** | Prohibited for core generation (narrow exceptions) | Mandatory in Methods section | No AI-generated images | Yes |
| **Wiley** | Permitted; authors accountable for IP | Mandatory in Methods or Acknowledgements | Not explicitly specified | Yes |
| **Taylor & Francis** | Permitted; authors assume full accountability | Mandatory acknowledgement with tool name + purpose | No AI-generated images | Yes |
| **JAIGP** | Expected (the entire paper may be AI-generated) | Mandatory HAI Card or equivalent | Not explicitly restricted | N/A (AI review is part of the pipeline) |

#### 7.2 Compliance Matrix

| Publisher Requirement | HAI Card Section | How It Is Satisfied |
|---|---|---|
| Identify AI tool(s) used | §4 (Models Used) | Every model name, role, and call count is listed. |
| Describe purpose of AI use | §2 (Generation History) | Each cycle row shows the action performed. |
| Human accountability statement | §5 (Human Review Status) | The "PENDING" status makes clear that no human has taken responsibility. If a human has reviewed, their identity and decision are recorded. |
| Verify cited references | §3 (Citation Summary) | Itemized counts of verified vs. failed citations. |
| Account for AI biases | §8 (Security Guarantee) | The security guarantee explicitly states what the chain does and does not prove, including the limitation that the Verifier is probabilistic and may miss errors. |
| Separate AI declaration | §1–§9 (entire card) | The HAI Card IS the AI declaration. It can be submitted as the "separate statement" required by Elsevier. |
| Link to raw prompts/outputs | §9 (Accountability Ledger) | Direct link to the workspace containing all prompts, responses, and think traces. |
| Confirm no AI-generated images | N/A | The factory does not generate images (D-0 §9, D-4 §4 EX-7). If the output paper contains figures, they must be human-created or generated by deterministic plotting code. The HAI Card does not claim otherwise. |
| Confirm no AI peer review use | §1 (Disclaimer) | The factory's Verifier is an internal pre-screening filter, not a peer review simulation. The HAI Card does not claim that the output has been "peer reviewed." |

#### 7.3 JAIGP-Specific Fields

| JAIGP Requirement | HAI Card Section | Notes |
|---|---|---|

### §8. The Autonomous Mathematics Research Levels

Feng et al. (2026b, §6.1) propose a two-axis taxonomy for
classifying AI-assisted mathematics:

**Axis 1: Level of Autonomy**

| Level | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| H | Primarily Human | Core content is human-generated; AI is auxiliary. |
| C | Human-AI Collaboration | Both parties contribute essentially. |
| A | Essentially Autonomous | Core content is fully AI-generated without essential human intervention. |

**Axis 2: Mathematical Significance**

| Level | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Negligible Novelty | Suitable for Olympiad/PhD exercises. |
| 1 | Minor Novelty | New result, but techniques don't meet publication standard. |
| 2 | Publication Grade | Publishable in a research journal following peer review. |
| 3 | Major Advance | Highest level of typical human results; top-5 journals. |
| 4 | Landmark Breakthrough | Generational importance. |

**Where does our factory's output fall?**

All factory output is, by definition, **Level A** (Essentially
Autonomous) on the autonomy axis: the human provides only the
research brief.

On the significance axis, the factory's output is expected to
fall at **Level 0** (Negligible Novelty) until the models
improve. The "slop" designation is an honest statement that
we expect Level 0. If a run produces something that a domain
expert later evaluates as Level 1 or above, that is a
pleasant surprise, not a design goal.

**The HAI Card does NOT attempt to self-assign a significance
level.** Per Feng et al. (2026b, §6.1.2): "the Level of
Mathematical Significance can only be evaluated by
mathematicians." The factory has no mechanism to assess
whether its output is trivial or significant. It always
reports the autonomy level as "A" and leaves the significance
level blank, to be filled by a human reviewer.

The HAI Card includes a field for this:
Autonomous Mathematics Research Level (Feng et al., 2026b)

AxisValueAutonomyA (Essentially Autonomous)Significance[TO BE ASSESSED BY HUMAN DOMAIN EXPERT]

### §9. HAI Card for Non-Standard Run Outcomes

The factory produces four types of terminal states (D-2 §3.3).
Each requires a different HAI Card rendering.

#### 9.1 `COMPLETED` (Normal)

Standard rendering as described in §3–§4.

#### 9.2 `NO_OUTPUT`

The Generation History table contains one row (the Generator's
`NO_OUTPUT` declaration). The Final Outcome section shows:
3. Final Outcome


FieldValueFinal VerdictNO_OUTPUTFinal ConfidenceN/ATotal Cycles0 (Generator declared inability)

{no_output_explanation}

This declaration is sealed in the provenance chain as a
legitimate artifact. The system correctly identified its

but unreliable content.
**Why document NO_OUTPUT runs?** Per Feng et al. (2026b,

### §10. Relationship to Other Output Artifacts

The HAI Card is one of four output artifacts (D-0 §9). Their
relationship:
```plaintext
                ┌──────────────┐
                │ paper.md     │  The output text
                │ (with YAML   │  (what the reader reads)
                │  header)     │
                └──────┬───────┘
                       │ references
                ┌──────▼───────┐
                │ hai_card.md  │  The disclosure
                │              │  (what the reviewer reads)
                └──────┬───────┘
                       │ references
                ┌──────▼───────┐
                │ manifest.json│  The technical proof
                │              │  (what the auditor reads)
                └──────┬───────┘
                       │ contains
                ┌──────▼───────┐
                │ provenance   │  The visualization
                │ _report.html │  (what anyone can read)
                └──────────────┘
```
**The HAI Card is the bridge between the paper and the
manifest.** A reader who does not understand hash chains
reads the HAI Card. A reader who wants cryptographic proof
follows the HAI Card's links to the manifest and the
verification tool.

The paper's YAML header (D-0 §9.1) cross-references the HAI
Card:

```yaml
hai_card: "./hai_card.md"
```

The HAI Card cross-references the manifest:

```plaintext
Provenance manifest: ./chain/manifest.json
The manifest contains the HAI Card's hash:
"hai_card_hash": "a3b1f9..."
```

This triple cross-reference ensures that no artifact can be
substituted without breaking the linkage. Replacing the HAI
Card changes its hash, which no longer matches the manifest.
Replacing the manifest breaks the chain. Replacing the paper
breaks the output_hash in the manifest.

### §11. Post-Generation Human Modifications

After the factory completes and the HAI Card is generated,
the human operator may wish to modify the HAI Card — for
example, to fill in the `public_repository_url`, to add a
significance level assessment, or to note that a domain expert
has reviewed the output.

**Procedure for modifying the HAI Card after generation:**

1. Edit `hai_card.md` in the workspace.
2. Add a modification note at the bottom:
Post-Generation Modifications


[{date}] {description of modification}
Modified by: {name or ORCID}


3. Re-seal the modified HAI Card:
slop-cli seal --file hai_card.md 
--prev-hash {manifest_seal_hash}
4. Append the new seal receipt to the chain directory.

The re-sealed HAI Card creates a chain extension beyond the
manifest seal. This is by design: the original manifest
remains valid (it references the original HAI Card hash),
and the new seal proves when and how the modification
occurred.

This procedure is necessary for the common case where a
human reviewer assesses the output after the factory completes
and wants to record their assessment in the HAI Card.

### §13. Evolution Path

As the HAI Card standard matures and the community provides
feedback, the following extensions are anticipated:

| Extension | Motivation | Dependency |
|---|---|---|
| Machine-readable HAI Card (JSON-LD or Schema.org) | Enable automated processing by journal submission systems, JAIGP's AI screener, and search engines. | Community adoption of a structured HAI Card schema. |
| Significance level auto-suggestion | A future version of the Verifier could suggest (not assert) a significance level based on novelty search results. The suggestion would be clearly labeled as "AI-estimated" and require human confirmation. | Improved NV-2 task (D-4 §3.4). |
| Multi-run comparison card | When a researcher runs the factory multiple times on the same brief, a summary card showing how the output evolved across runs. | Run registry (D-1 §6, Non-Defense 3). |
| Interactive HTML HAI Card | A web-based version where each cycle row is expandable to show the full prompt, response, and think trace inline. | The provenance report (D-0 §9.4) partially serves this function already. |
| Publisher-specific card variants | Auto-generate Elsevier's "AI declaration statement" or Springer Nature's "Methods section AI disclosure" directly from the HAI Card data. | Stable publisher policy APIs or templates (currently manual). |
