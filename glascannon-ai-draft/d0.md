## Part II: The Blueprint

# ANTIPHORIA SLOP-RESEARCH-FACTORY

## System Blueprint v0.1

### Document Purpose and Intended Readers

This document is the master architectural plan for the **antiphoria-research-factory**, an open-source implementation of the **Generator → Verifier → Reviser** loop for autonomous scientific text production, coupled with a cryptographic provenance engine that seals every step of the process into a tamper-evident chain.

- **Primary audience:** LLM coding assistants (Claude, Cursor, Copilot) that will implement the system component by component, under human review at every stage.
- **Secondary audience:** Human developers and researchers reviewing the plan before authorizing implementation.
- **What this document decides:** The overall architecture, component boundaries, data flow topology, model assignments, provenance integration points, and the explicit boundaries of what the system claims to do versus what it actually does.
- **What this document does NOT contain:** Code, exact prompt text, deployment configurations, or security audits. Those belong to downstream documents D-1 through D-9.

---

## §1. Project Identity: What This Is, Honestly

### 1.1 The Name

"Slop-Research-Factory" is not ironic. It is a load-bearing engineering term.

> **Slop** *(noun, technical):* AI-generated text that exhibits the surface structure of rigorous academic writing — correct LaTeX formatting, plausible citations, coherent argumentation — while lacking the epistemic grounding that makes real research real. Slop may contain hallucinated references, subtly invalid logical steps, or claims that no domain expert has verified. It is structurally indistinguishable from genuine research to a non-expert reader.

The factory produces slop **knowingly and transparently**. Every artifact it generates carries a cryptographic manifest declaring: *"This was produced by an autonomous LLM pipeline. No human has verified the scientific claims. The complete reasoning chain is sealed and auditable."*

### 1.2 Why Build a Factory That Produces Slop

Three reasons, in order of importance:

1. **Reason 1 — Provenance demonstration.** The real product is not the papers. The real product is the provenance framework. The factory exists to stress-test whether a cryptographic seal-chain can provide meaningful auditability for multi-step AI reasoning workflows. The slop is the test payload.

2. **Reason 2 — Public discourse catalyst.** When a tool like this becomes freely available — and it will, whether we build it or someone else does — the academic community, publishers, and the public need to have already debated its implications. Releasing a well-documented, honest, Glass Box implementation forces that conversation. The alternative is that these tools arrive as black boxes with no provenance at all.

3. **Reason 3 — The science-fiction horizon.** Google DeepMind's Aletheia, powered by Gemini 3 Deep Think and supported by dozens of expert mathematicians, solved 6 out of 10 FirstProof research-level problems autonomously in February 2026. This is the trajectory:

| Date | System | Achievement | Human Input | Compute Budget |
|------|--------|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| Jul 2025 | Gemini Deep Think | IMO Gold Medal (5/6 problems) | Zero | Large |
| Dec 2025 | Aletheia (Nov base) | 4 open Erdős problems; 6.5% meaningful accuracy on 700 | Semi-autonomous grading | Very Large |
| Feb 2026 | Aletheia (Feb base) | 6/10 FirstProof; publication-grade proof of Problem 7 | Zero for generation; expert for evaluation | Extremely Large |

Extrapolate forward 2–5 years. Assume open-weights models close 60% of the gap with Gemini Deep Think. Assume inference costs drop 10× per year. The slop-research-factory's architecture **is** the architecture of a real research factory. The difference is the model quality feeding it. When the models arrive, the plumbing must already exist.

### 1.3 The Aletheia Papers as Theoretical North Star

The system's loop topology is derived directly from two Google DeepMind publications:

- **"Towards Autonomous Mathematics Research"** (Feng et al., 2026b, arXiv:2602.10177v3)  — introduces Aletheia, the Generator-Verifier-Reviser architecture, inference-time scaling laws, Human-AI Interaction Cards, and Autonomous Mathematics Research Levels.
- **"Aletheia tackles FirstProof autonomously"** (Feng et al., 2026a, arXiv:2602.21201v3)  — provides raw prompts, outputs, and the Verification-and-Extraction prompt used to process Aletheia's outputs.

Key architectural principles extracted from these papers:

| Aletheia Principle | Our Implementation |
|---|---|
| Generator-Verifier-Reviser cyclic loop | LangGraph directed cyclic graph with conditional edges |
| Self-filtering: "No solution found" as a valid output | Explicit `NO_OUTPUT` state; the factory must be able to declare failure |
| Verification-and-Extraction prompt (Appendix A of 2602.21201v3) | A dedicated Verifier node with a structured critique → verdict → resolution pipeline |
| Verdicts: `[CORRECT]`, `[WRONG]`, `[FIXABLE]` | Pydantic enum output from the Verifier, not a binary boolean |
| HAI Card for transparency | Auto-generated HAI Card as a mandatory output artifact |
| Inference-time scaling as primary lever | Configurable compute budget per node, tracked in state |
| `

### 1.4 What This System Does NOT Claim

**The system does not claim to:**

- Produce correct scientific research
- Replace human peer review
- Produce output suitable for submission to traditional journals without full human verification
- Detect all hallucinations
- Guarantee that citations are valid beyond surface-level existence checks
- Compete with Aletheia (which uses Gemini 3 Deep Think and has access to billions of dollars of compute)

**The system does claim to:**

- Produce the most transparent, auditable AI-generated research text that current open-source technology permits
- Provide a cryptographic proof of exactly how every sentence was generated
- Implement the Aletheia loop pattern faithfully, using the best available open-weights models
- Serve as a reference architecture for the community to study, critique, and improve

## §2. Architecture Overview

### 2.1 The Five Layers

The system is organized into five layers. Every implementation task belongs to exactly one layer.
```plaintext
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Layer 5: OUTPUT ARTIFACTS                              │
│  (PDF, Markdown, HAI Card, C2PA Manifest)               │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Layer 4: PROVENANCE ENGINE                             │
│  (slop-cli seal, hash chain, manifest compilation)      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Layer 3: THE ALETHEIA LOOP                             │
│  (Generator → Verifier → Reviser, conditional routing)  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Layer 2: INFERENCE MIDDLEWARE                           │
│  (LiteLLM, Instructor/Pydantic, Tavily, async I/O)     │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Layer 1: ORCHESTRATION & STATE                         │
│  (LangGraph or hand-rolled state machine, FactoryState) │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```
**Critical constraint:** Each layer communicates with adjacent layers only through explicitly defined interfaces. Layer 4 (Provenance) must be usable independently of Layer 3 (the Loop). Layer 3 must be usable without Layer 4. This ensures the provenance engine is a portable library, not a framework lock-in.

### 2.2 The Aletheia Loop as a Directed Cyclic Graph

The core execution topology:
```plaintext
                ┌──────────────┐
                │  ENTRY POINT │
                │  (Research   │
                │   Brief)     │
                └──────┬───────┘
                       │
                       ▼
                ┌──────────────┐
          ┌────▶│  GENERATOR   │
          │     │  (Draft)     │
          │     └──────┬───────┘
          │            │
          │            ▼
          │     ┌──────────────┐
          │     │   VERIFIER   │
          │     │  (Critique)  │
          │     └──────┬───────┘
          │            │
          │            ├── verdict == CORRECT ──▶ FINALIZE
          │            │
          │            ├── verdict == FIXABLE ──▶ REVISER ──┐
          │            │                                     │
          │            │                          ┌──────────┘
          │            │                          │
          │            │                          ▼
          │            │                   ┌──────────────┐
          │            │                   │   REVISER    │
          │            │                   │  (Revision)  │
          │            │                   └──────┬───────┘
          │            │                          │
          │            │                          │
          │            └── verdict == WRONG ──▶ GENERATOR (retry)
          │                                       │
          └───────────────────────────────────────┘
          (only if rejection_count < MAX_REJECTIONS)
          
          If rejection_count >= MAX_REJECTIONS:
                       │
                       ▼
                ┌──────────────┐
                │ HUMAN RESCUE │
                │   QUEUE      │
                └──────────────┘
```
**Three verdicts, not two.** Following Aletheia's Verification-and-Extraction prompt (Appendix A of 2602.21201v3), the Verifier outputs one of three verdicts:

| Verdict | Meaning | Graph Action |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| `CORRECT` | "The solution is flawless, completely rigorous, and requires no changes." | → FINALIZE node |
| `FIXABLE` | "The core approach is sound, but it contains minor errors, skips necessary steps, or lacks formal academic rigor." | → REVISER node (with the Verifier's critique injected into the Reviser's prompt) |
| `WRONG` | "The solution is fundamentally flawed, relies on invalid logic, or cannot be salvaged without a complete rewrite." | → GENERATOR node (full retry), increment `rejection_count` |

The **FINALIZE** node does not simply output text. It:

1. Compiles the full hash chain into a C2PA-compatible manifest
2. Generates the HAI Card from the FactoryState
3. Writes the final Markdown/PDF with embedded provenance metadata

### 2.3 What Happens Inside Each Node

Every node in the graph executes the same four-phase protocol. This is the **Glass Box Completeness Invariant**.
Phase 1: PRE-SEAL (before any LLM call)
→ Write intent JSON to workspace (contains: step_index, node_name,
prompt_to_be_sent, latest_hash, timestamp)
→ Call provenance engine: seal(intent_file) → new_hash
→ Update state.latest_hash
Phase 2: INFERENCE (the actual LLM call)
→ Call LiteLLM with the configured model
→ Capture BOTH the  reasoning trace AND the final output
→ Write raw response (including think tokens) to workspace
Phase 3: POST-SEAL (after inference completes)
→ Write outcome JSON to workspace (contains: step_index, node_name,
model_used, token_counts, response_hash, latest_hash)
→ Call provenance engine: seal(outcome_file, prev_hash=latest_hash) → new_hash
→ Update state.latest_hash
Phase 4: YIELD (return control to the graph)
→ Increment state.step_index
→ Append to state.messages
→ Return updated FactoryState to LangGraph
**This protocol is non-negotiable.** If any phase fails (e.g., the LLM API times out during Phase 2), the node must not proceed to Phase 3. The incomplete step is logged, and the graph retries from Phase 1 of the same node. The hash chain never contains a dangling, unsealed inference.

## §3. State Schema: The FactoryState

The entire system's coherence depends on a single, strictly typed state object that flows through every node.

### Canonical fields:

| Field | Type | Mutability | Purpose |
|-------|------|------------|---------|
| `run_id` | `str (UUID4)` | Immutable | Unique identifier for this generation run |
| `research_brief` | `ResearchBrief` | Immutable | The human-provided input (thesis, scope, key references) |
| `step_index` | `int` | Increment-only | Monotonically increasing counter, never decremented |
| `latest_hash` | `str` | Write-after-seal-only | The most recent cryptographic hash in the chain |
| `workspace` | `Path` | Immutable | Absolute path to this run's workspace directory |
| `current_draft` | `str` | Replaced per cycle | The latest version of the generated text |
| `think_trace` | `str` | Replaced per cycle | The `<think>` reasoning tokens from the most recent Generator/Reviser call |
| `verifier_critique` | `VerifierOutput \| None` | Replaced per cycle | The structured Pydantic output from the most recent Verifier call |
| `rejection_count` | `int` | Increment-only | Number of WRONG verdicts received; triggers human rescue at threshold |
| `revision_count` | `int` | Increment-only | Number of FIXABLE verdicts received; tracked separately from rejections |
| `messages` | `list[Message]` | Append-only | Complete, ordered history of all prompts and responses |
| `discovered_citations` | `list[CitationCheckResult]` | Append-only | Results of every citation verification performed by the Verifier |
| `sealed_steps` | `list[SealedStepReceipt]` | Append-only | Receipt for every pre-seal and post-seal operation |
| `status` | `Literal["running", "completed", "failed", "human_rescue"]` | Transitions forward only | Current lifecycle state |

### Derived types (to be fully specified in D-2):

| Type | Key Fields |
|------|------------|
| `ResearchBrief` | `title`, `thesis_statement`, `scope_constraints`, `key_references: list[str]`, `target_length_words: int` |
| `VerifierOutput` | `verdict: Literal["CORRECT", "FIXABLE", "WRONG"]`, `critique: str`, `failed_citations: list[CitationCheckResult]`, `logical_issues: list[str]`, `confidence: float (0.0–1.0)` |
| `CitationCheckResult` | `claimed_reference: str`, `check_method: Literal["doi_lookup", "semantic_scholar", "tavily_search", "not_checked"]`, `exists: bool \| None`, `metadata_match: bool \| None`, `relevance_assessment: str \| None` |
| `SealedStepReceipt` | `step_index: int`, `phase: Literal["pre_seal", "post_seal"]`, `file_path: Path`, `hash: str`, `prev_hash: str`, `timestamp: datetime` |
| `Message` | `role: Literal["system", "user", "assistant", "tool"]`, `content: str`, `node_name: str`, `step_index: int` |

**Design rationale for `confidence: float` instead of `is_hallucinated: bool`:** The Aletheia papers demonstrate that even expert human mathematicians disagree on correctness (Problem 8 received a 5/7 split). Forcing a binary boolean creates false certainty. The `confidence` field records the Verifier's self-assessed certainty, which the routing logic can threshold differently for different use cases.

## §4. Model Topology

### 4.1 Default Configuration (Cost-Optimized)

| Role | Node(s) | Model | Access Method | Justification |
|------|---------|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Generator | Generator, Reviser | `deepseek/deepseek-r1` | OpenRouter via LiteLLM | Best open-weights reasoning model at time of writing. Captures `<think>` tokens. Near-o1 performance on mathematical reasoning at ~2% of the cost. |
| Verifier | Verifier | `google/gemini-2.5-flash` | Google AI API via LiteLLM | Massive context window (1M tokens). Fast time-to-first-token. Optimized for retrieval and classification, not generation. Cost: ~$0.15/M input tokens. |
| Fallback/Local | Any | `ollama/deepseek-r1:32b` or equivalent | Local Ollama | For development, testing, and environments without API access. Provenance chain still enforced identically. |

### 4.2 Why the Generator and Reviser Use the Same Model

The Reviser is not a different cognitive function from the Generator. It is the Generator operating under an additional constraint: the Verifier's structured critique. The Reviser's system prompt is dynamically constructed as:
[Generator's base system prompt]



[Verifier's critique, verbatim]
+
[Instruction: "Revise the draft to address every issue identified above.
Do not discard sound reasoning. Fix what is broken.
The Verifier's critique hash is: {critique_hash}"]
The critique hash is injected so that the sealed output can be cryptographically linked to the specific critique that motivated the revision.

### 4.3 The `<think>` Token Capture Requirement

DeepSeek-R1 and similar reasoning models emit extended `<think>...
</details>` token sequences before producing their final answer. These tokens contain the model's internal scratchpad: dead ends, self-corrections, alternative approaches considered and rejected.

**The Glass Box demands that we seal the scratchpad, not just the final output.**

Implementation requirement for LiteLLM configuration:

- The middleware must be configured to return the full response including `<think>` tokens
- The `think_trace` field in FactoryState stores these tokens separately from `current_draft`
- Both are written to the workspace and sealed in Phase 3 (POST-SEAL)
- The final output artifact includes the think trace as a collapsible appendix

This mirrors Aletheia's practice: *"We report the performance of Aletheia... Raw prompts and outputs are available at [GitHub]."*

## §5. Provenance Engine Integration

### 5.1 Assumption: The Engine Exists

This blueprint assumes a working command-line tool (referred to as `slop-cli` or equivalent) that provides three operations:

| Operation | Input | Output | Guarantee |
|-----------|-------|--------|-----------|
| `begin_chain(run_id)` | Run identifier | Genesis receipt (initial hash) | Creates the root of a new hash chain |
| `seal(file_path, prev_hash)` | Path to file + previous hash | `SealedStepReceipt` (new hash, timestamp, file digest) | The new hash commits to both the file contents and the previous hash, forming a chain |
| `verify_chain(workspace_path)` | Path to workspace directory | Verification report (pass/fail per step, chain integrity) | An independent third party can run this and confirm the chain is unbroken |

### 5.2 Integration Points

The provenance engine is called exactly **twice per node execution** (pre-seal and post-seal), plus once at finalization (manifest compilation).

For a typical 3-cycle run (Generator → Verifier → Reviser → Verifier → Reviser → Verifier → Finalize), the chain contains:
genesis
```plaintext
→ pre_seal(generator_intent)
→ post_seal(generator_output + think_trace)
→ pre_seal(verifier_intent)
→ post_seal(verifier_critique)         [verdict: FIXABLE]
→ pre_seal(reviser_intent + critique)
→ post_seal(reviser_output + think_trace)
→ pre_seal(verifier_intent_v2)
→ post_seal(verifier_critique_v2)       [verdict: FIXABLE]
→ pre_seal(reviser_intent_v2 + critique_v2)
→ post_seal(reviser_output_v2 + think_trace_v2)
→ pre_seal(verifier_intent_v3)
→ post_seal(verifier_critique_v3)       [verdict: CORRECT]
→ manifest_seal(compiled_manifest)
```

That is **13 seals** for a 3-cycle run. Each seal is a disk write + hash computation. At ~1ms per seal, the provenance overhead is negligible relative to LLM inference time (typically 30–300 seconds per call).

### 5.3 Manifest Compilation

The FINALIZE node compiles the full chain into a single manifest file (JSON or CBOR) that can be:

1. Embedded as metadata in the output PDF
2. Published alongside the output as a sidecar file
3. Mapped onto C2PA Content Credential structure (detailed in D-5)

The manifest includes:

- The complete ordered list of `SealedStepReceipt` objects
- The `ResearchBrief` (the human input that started the run)
- Model identifiers and versions for every inference call
- Token counts (input/output/think) for every inference call
- The final `VerifierOutput` that triggered the CORRECT verdict
- The auto-generated HAI Card

## §6. The Verifier: Design Principles

The Verifier is the single most important node in the system. If the Verifier is unreliable, the entire loop produces confidently sealed garbage.

### 6.1 Honest Capability Assessment

Drawing from the Aletheia papers and independent framework evaluations, here is what an LLM-based Verifier can and cannot do:

| Verification Task | Feasibility | Method | Confidence Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Does a cited paper exist (DOI/URL check)? | High | HTTP HEAD request or Crossref API. No LLM needed. | Deterministic |
| Does citation metadata match (author, year, title)? | High | Semantic Scholar or Crossref API lookup. | Deterministic |
| Does the cited paper actually support the claim attributed to it? | Medium | LLM with retrieved abstract. Probabilistic. | Low-Medium |
| Is the mathematical reasoning valid? | Low-Medium | LLM chain-of-thought verification. Prone to the same errors as generation. | Low |
| Is the argumentation logically sound? | Low | LLM judgment. Cannot reliably distinguish subtle fallacies from valid reasoning. | Very Low |
| Is the claim novel? | Very Low | Would require knowing the entire state of the field. | Not attempted |

**Design consequence:** The Verifier outputs a graduated confidence assessment, not a binary pass/fail on each dimension. The routing logic uses configurable thresholds. A conservative configuration routes to FIXABLE on any confidence < 0.8. An aggressive configuration may accept confidence > 0.6.

### 6.2 The Verification-and-Extraction Prompt

Modeled directly on Aletheia's Appendix A (2602.21201v3), the Verifier's prompt follows this structure:

```plaintext
INDEPENDENT VERIFICATION
"Before evaluating the candidate, independently analyze the research
brief to determine the correct methodology and potential edge cases.
Then, do a line-by-line verification of the draft."


CRITIQUE
"Point out any specific flaws, leaps in logic, or informalities.
Check all citations for existence and relevance."


VERDICT
Exactly one of: [CORRECT], [FIXABLE], [WRONG]


RESOLUTION


If CORRECT: State why.


If WRONG: Detail the fatal flaw. Do NOT rewrite.


If FIXABLE: Generate a complete, corrected version OR
a detailed remediation plan for the Reviser.
```
The exact prompt text will be specified in D-3. The key structural requirement is that the Verifier must produce the **critique before the verdict**, forcing it to commit to specific observations before making a judgment call.
